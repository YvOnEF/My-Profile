Project Idea

Overview

This will be an app that provides statistics and feedback on source credibility and bias. The app will access the user’s Chrome browsing history and YouTube watching history. The software will evaluate these sources and give a report on the credibility and bias of them. This app is designed to combat the effect of different algorithms that display articles and videos that users like to see and lead users’ using experience to the extreme. Ultimately, this will cause users to be isolated from different opinions and the truth. By using this app and getting the feedback from the reports, users can avoid getting isolated by these algorithms and get a better browsing experience. However, there is a disadvantage of such an app: accessing user data and browsing history may cause privacy concerns for some.

Motivation

According to safari digital, more than 86.5% of Australians are online with the average daily usage time exceedingly more than 3 hours each day. There are about 21.93 million active Australian Internet users each month. Therefore, people will be in great demand for this piece of software. The app I’m going to design will be presented simply and follow the guidelines of a normal app. It will have features that can access tools of the mobile (considering it must be touchscreen) like the camera or web browser, and the network. The application’s algorithm will focus on analysing the news and articles on the Internet. It provides statistical feedback on source credibility and bias, doesn’t leak user privacy and doesn’t interfere with user judgment, and does apply to a broader user audience. The advantage of this app is that it won’t interfere with a user's judgment by blocking information and this app is a great solution to solve the problem. It only provides statistical data as a reference and provide more information to help users to make judgments if the contents are credible or not by themselves.

Description

For the primary features, the app will provide feedback on users’ browsing history. The application will access the user's browsing history such as web browsing history and YouTube watching history and will evaluate the credibility of the source and provide statistical feedback. For example, one of the feedbacks is that the app will show a bar chart with two columns. One indicates the percentage of unbiased sources; the other bar chart indicates the percentage of biased sources and unbiased sources. The users then can have a visualization of what level of bias is the information he/she usually browses. The benefit of the feature is that it helps prevent people from being trapped by filter bubbles unconsciously and let people be aware of the misinformation. The app will also provide a credibility score. The application can take a picture or a video of any news interface and give a score to inform the users whether that news or article is credible. For instance, if a user wants to verify the credibility of an Instagram post, he/she can take a screenshot, and upload the photo to the application, then the application will give a score ranging from 0 to 100, which 100 represents credited news and 0 represents fake news. The benefit of the score credibility is that it avoids absolute binary answers which are wrong or right. This keeps the application neutral and unbiased and stimulates the users to think critically. For example, if a news item is 90% credited, the user might want to read more relevant news to verify the remaining 10%.

For the secondary features, the app has the function of content sharing. Within the application, users can send and share the content of information to whoever they would like to send it to. This ensures the participation of users across a larger audience and become much more convenient to share. Additionally, citation tool is another functionality of the app. If a user has some questions regarding an article or news page, our application will have a small feature that you can tap on to get the publication of that source. The application will reach out and find the correct credentials, citations, authors, etc. Furthermore, the app also has the database storage. Some users may want to save a source for future reading, or for reference. The information of what content is being saved can be applied to the algorithm which can help the developers understand the type of content users liked to have saved on their devices.
